BREACH

FIELD MANUAL — EVERYTHING FROM FIRST DEPLOY TO FULL LEVEL LOGIC

BREACH is a browser FPS with a complete in-game level editor. This manual covers both halves: how to fight, and how to build — props, physics, locked doors, signal logic, cinematics, and sharing what you make. Every section below collapses, just like the editor panel itself.

QUICK START
1
Open the game and press Deploy. You spawn with a rifle; enemies arrive in waves.
2
Survive the wave, collect coins and pickups, pick an upgrade between waves, repeat. The objective banner at the top tells you the win condition for the current level.
3
Press P any time mid-run to open the level editor and reshape the world you are standing in. Press P again (or ▶ Play level) to jump back in.

The HUD shows health and ammo bottom-center, credits and wave info top, grenades and buffs near your health, and a minimap. Key items you are carrying appear as colored chips bottom-left.

COMBAT & MOVEMENT

Weapons

Rifle (starting, accurate auto), SMG (fast, big mag), Shotgun (pellets, close range), Sniper (scoped, huge damage), Rocket launcher (splash), Crowbar (melee), plus grenades. Refill ammo at supply stations with credits (E), from ammo pickups, or in loot caches. Headshots hit harder.

Movement tech

  • Slide — sprint, then crouch. Keeps speed, lowers your profile.
  • Crouch is physical — your collision capsule and hit volume genuinely shrink. Use low cover and gaps; enemies aim at your actual chest height, crouched or standing, on flat ground or hills.
  • Grab & throw — physics props can be carried (G) and thrown. Heavy props hurt what they land on.

Destruction

Breakable props shatter into debris; explosive props (think gas cans) detonate with grenade-style splash — shoot them near enemies, not near yourself.

GAME MODES

Solo / co-op waves

The default mode: enemy waves with an objective that the level author chooses — clear N waves, survival (outlast the clock), defend (hold a zone), destroy (kill marked objective props), escort (walk the payload), or extraction (reach the green ring). Between waves you pick a run upgrade.

PvP

Duel (1v1, first to the kill target), FFA, TDM (red vs blue), and Control point (king-of-the-hill capture). The host can fill matches with bots at four skill tiers — Recruit, Regular, Veteran, Elite — which tune their speed, aim, fire rate, and durability.

MULTIPLAYER
1
Host a game from the menu — you get a room code (your peer id).
2
Friends choose Join and paste the code. Connections are direct peer-to-peer; nothing to install or host.
3
The host’s level — including all logic, keys, and signals — is pushed to everyone automatically.

The host owns the simulation (enemies, waves, coins, win conditions); everyone runs their own movement locally so it feels instant. Each player has their own key ring, but once anyone unlocks a door it stays unlocked for the whole team.

CAUTION — P2P connections occasionally fail on strict networks (NAT). If a join hangs, both players retrying or switching networks usually resolves it.
EDITOR BASICS

Press P to open the editor. The panel docks right (swap sides with ⇄, drag the edge to resize, collapse with ⌄). Click objects to select; SHIFT+click multi-selects props. Move / Rotate / Scale with the gizmo handles or type exact values.

The ＋ button

The floating circle beside the panel adds anything placeable: shapes, a light, an enemy spawn, audio zones, and pickup pads (with a submenu to choose exactly which pad — keys included). Whatever you add, the editor jumps to the right tab so you are looking at it.

Heavy tool groups (Mechanism, Waypoint path, Physics & destruction, Signals) are collapsed bars in the inspector — click to open; the editor remembers which you keep open.

PROPS & BUILDING

Shapes & models

Drop primitive shapes (box, sphere, cylinder, cone) and restyle them in Material — color, texture with tiling, emissive glow. For real models, paste any GLB URL or search the built-in 🔍 Poly Pizza library. Models with animations can auto-loop or be set to E Activate (trigger on interact).

Lights

Add point lights, tune color / intensity / distance, and drag them with the gizmo. Markers only show while editing.

TIP — Repeated identical static shapes are automatically instanced for performance when you play, and split back apart when you edit.
MECHANISMS & PATHS

Any prop can become a mechanism — a door, platform, or elevator. Open the Mechanism bar on a selected prop and give it a move offset and/or rotation, a duration, a mode, and a trigger:

  • Mode — Loop (back and forth forever), or Once (travels and locks at the destination; a fired Once stops offering its E prompt).
  • Trigger — Auto (always running), E Activate (a player toggles it), or Signals only (never autoplays, shows no prompt, and can’t be E’d — it moves exclusively when a signal drives it; tag it so signals can find it).
  • Waypoint path — instead of a single offset, trace a route: move yourself, add waypoints, and the prop ping-pongs through them. Players ride moving platforms.

Mechanism state is mirrored to every player in multiplayer — one person opens the door, everyone sees it open.

PHYSICS & DESTRUCTION

The Physics & destruction bar on a selected prop controls how it behaves under fire:

  • Dynamic — falls, tumbles, stacks, reacts to shots. Set its mass.
  • Grabbable — players can carry and throw it.
  • Breakable — HP, then it shatters (jagged shards or clean cubes; tune fragment count and size) or puffs.
  • 🎯 Objective target — counts toward the destroy objective.
  • 💥 Explosive — detonates when destroyed: blast radius, damage, and impact-detonation velocity.
KEYS & LOCKS

Gate any interactable behind a key:

1
Place a key pad — ＋ → Pickup pad ▸ → a key (REDBLUEGOLDGREEN). Key pads are one-shot per run — they never respawn.
2
Select the door / lever / mechanism and set its Lock dropdown to the matching key. Optionally check Consume key on unlock.
3
In play, the prompt reads LOCKED — needs RED KEY until the player holds it, then E unlocks. Unlocks last for the current deploy and apply to the whole team; everything re-locks on the next deploy.

Custom names & models

In the Pickups module, select a key kind to reveal a Key name field — rename RED KEY to Engine Room Keycard and that name appears on the pad toast, the prompt, the HUD chip, and the Lock dropdown. Below it, paste a GLB URL to replace the floating diamond with your own model.

SIGNALS & LOGIC

Signals let props affect other props — the puzzle layer. Two halves, both in the Signals bar of the inspector:

  • Tag — name a prop (e.g. vaultDoor) so signals can find it. Several props can share one tag.
  • Signal rules — when this prop fires (On destroyed / On E), do something (Toggle / Open / Close a mechanism, Play anim (optionally a single named clip from the target’s GLB — a field appears under the rule; blank plays every clip), Unlock, Win level, or Play cutscene — which plays the level’s cinematic mid-game for every player and, like Win, needs no target) to every prop carrying the target tag.
  • Needs N — set on the target: it absorbs signals until N have arrived, then reacts. This is the “all of them” gate.

Worked example — the three generators

1
Build a vault door as a Once mechanism with trigger Signals only (so players can’t just walk up and E it). Tag it vault. Set Needs 3.
2
Place three breakable generator props. Give each one signal: On destroyed → Open → vault.
3
Optional finish: an exit prop inside with On E → Win level.

Mix sources freely — two destroyed crates plus a lever pull sum toward the same Needs counter. Counters reset every deploy.

CAUTION — In co-op, Win level fires when the host (or solo player) triggers it; a joining player’s E won’t end the match. All other actions work from anyone.
ENEMIES & SPAWNS

The Enemies tab covers who attacks and from where. Set per-type models, scale, colliders, and animation clips. Gunners fire re-aiming bursts; all enemies aim at your real (crouch- and terrain-aware) position.

Spawn points live here too: add one (＋ or the Add spawn button), drag it with the gizmo, and set its patrol radius and detect range — or trace a full patrol route. With spawns placed, waves use them; with none, waves spawn randomly.

PICKUPS

The Pickups module (Gameplay tab) manages every pad on the map: health, ammo, weapons, buffs (double damage, speed, shield), grenades, and keys. Pick a kind, walk to the spot, Place ahead. Each placed pad in the list has a dropdown — change what it is any time, no re-placing. Pads respawn on a 15-second timer; key pads are one-shot. Give any kind a custom model (URL + scale) to replace its floating icon. Turning Spawn pickups off hides everything while keeping your placements.

WORLD & AUDIO

World

Terrain (rugged hills with real collision), sky / HDR environments, fog, depth of field, and boundary walls all live in the World tab. Physics rebuilds automatically when you play.

Audio zones

Drop ambient sound bubbles: position, radius, volume, loop, and a sound URL — or hit Search Freesound to browse free CC audio in place. The ▶ on each result is a play/stop toggle. The same Freesound search appears anywhere audio is assignable: weapon shoot sounds, events, and the cutscene track.

CINEMATICS

Author cinematics in World → Cinematic. A level can hold several named cutscenes: cutscene #1 (named Intro by default) is the only one with the Play on deploy toggle; use the cutscene selector row (◂ name ▸, +, ✕) to add more, rename them in place, and play any of them mid-game from a signal’s Play cutscene action by typing its name (blank plays #1). Each cutscene carries its own audio track. A cutscene is one or more shots: use the Shot selector (◂ Shot 1 / N ▸, + Add shot, ✕) to chain takes back to back — each cut is a hard camera cut, perfect for establishing-shot-then-close-up sequences. Per shot: place camera path points, set duration (up to 120s each), lens (with an optional lens ramp), and rack focus; one audio track spans the whole sequence; animated props keep playing on camera during the cutscene — both GLB clip animations AND mechanism/waypoint motion (doors grinding open, platforms and elevators moving, props traveling their paths). Add a Cutscene audio track by URL or Freesound search. A dotted preview of the path shows while editing, with an arrow at every waypoint pointing exactly where the camera will face there during playback — blue when it follows the Look mode (spawn or path), gold when you’ve authored it. To author one: aim the editor view at your subject, then press the waypoint’s 👁 button (it captures whatever is under the view’s center); press ⊘ to clear it. The camera smoothly pans between authored looks during the shot, and waypoints without one keep the Look mode’s aim — the cinematic itself never plays inside the editor.

On deploy, the cinematic waits for the loading screen — your models and audio are fully loaded before the first frame, then it plays with letterbox bars and hands off to gameplay.

OBJECTIVES & RULES

The Gameplay tab sets the win condition (waves / survival / defend / destroy / escort / extraction), wave pacing, respawn rules, hit numbers, loot caches (and what they sell), and coin drops. Extraction marks a green ring — auto-placed opposite spawn, or drag it yourself.

SAVING & SHARING
  • 💾 Save — stores the level in your browser; it loads automatically next visit.
  • 🔗 Copy share link — a URL that opens your level for anyone.
  • ⬇ Export / ⬆ Import .json — the level as a file: back it up, hand-edit it, trade it.
  • Campaigns — chain levels into a sequence with carry-over loadouts and a finale screen.

Level check

Above the Save button, the editor reviews your level continuously: a lock with no matching key pad, an orphaned key, signals pointing at tags nothing carries, targetless signals, empty audio-zone URLs, a cinematic with no path. Warnings never block saving — they are there so a shared level plays the way you meant it.

TROUBLESHOOTING
SymptomLikely cause & fix
A door won’t openIt’s locked (check the prompt — you may need a key), it’s a fired Once mechanism (spent by design), or its Needs-N counter isn’t satisfied yet.
My signal does nothingCheck the Save tab’s Level check — the target tag probably doesn’t match any prop, or the target needs more signals.
A key door is open on a fresh runIt shouldn’t be — unlocks reset every deploy. Make sure you’re on the current build.
Multiplayer join hangsP2P/NAT issue — both retry, or try another network.
A model won’t loadThe URL must serve a GLB with CORS allowed; Poly Pizza results are pre-cleared.
Editor feels clutteredCollapse sections and the inspector bars — the editor remembers your layout. Headers and panel side/size are all yours to arrange.
